Research

As a member of the McQueen research group at Johns Hopkins University, my work is focused on manipulating electronic, magnetic, and topological properties of materials through precise control of crystal chemistry. To achieve this control, our group employs synthetic techniques that take place over a vast range of temperatures--from solution-based "soft" chemistry that gently and pushes and pulls ions into and out of a solid at room temperature, to extremely hot electrical arcs that melt metals together to form new alloys.

Here are some areas of research to which I have made substantial contributions:

  • Non-trivial surface topology and superconductivity in Tl5Te3
  • Superconductivity in Bismuth Oxy-Sulfides
  • Charge doping, defects, and structural disorder in Na2IrO3
I am also interested in the design of new battery materials and electrolytes, and my current research in this area focuses on understanding the kinetics of sodium-ion transport in batteries, both across the solid-liquid interface, and in solution. More to come on this...

A significant portion of my work requires data analysis and aggregation from many different sources, such as neutron diffraction, magnetometry, and optical spectroscopy, to uncover complex structure-property relationships in the materials I synthesize. In addition to tools like MS Excel and OriginPro, I frequently use Python for data manipulation and analysis. I've shared a few of my Python scripts and tutorials in the Resources section.

An "exploratory mission": 18 samples of slightly different chemical compositions were prepared and heated simultaneously in an attempt to find new, potentially useful superconducting materials. The drastic color differences between pellets illustrate the profound effect that even a small change in composition can have on a material's characteristics.
