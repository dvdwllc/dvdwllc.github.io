David C. Wallace, Ph.D.

I recently completed my graduate research at Johns Hopkins University, where I studied the magnetic behavior and chemical reactivity of iridium oxide honeycomb materials. Some of my work in this field was recently featured on the cover of Dalton Transactions.

After leaving JHU, I participated in an 8 week data science fellowship at The Data Incubator, where I sharpened my skills in machine learning, algorithms, natural language processing and distributed computing. I'm now headed to work as a Data Scientist at Amazon in Seattle!

Recent Projects 

Politivec

Using natural language processing and machine learning techniques, I've built a model that can estimate the political ideology of an arbitrary amount of text. To learn more about the model, check out my recent blog post!



Stomping Grounds

I built an intuitive way to aggregate and interpret geospatial datasets using kernel density estimation and unsupervised machine learning. Stomping Grounds combines a broad variety of datasets, such as criminal arrests and restaurant locations, to suggest areas of Baltimore city to prospective homebuyers and entrepreneurs.
