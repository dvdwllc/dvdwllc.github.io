About

I grew up in Skagit Valley, Washington, where my family has owned and operated a potato farm for four generations. As a young lad I cultivated interests in science, mathematics, computers, and mechanics by designing and constructing skateboard ramps, building PC's, and working on motorcycles and farm equipment. After graduating from Burlington-Edison high school, I attended Whitman College in Walla Walla, Washington, where I majored in Chemistry. As a Whitman student, I played lead trombone in several ensembles and campus bands, served two terms on the interfraternity counsel as treasurer and president, and also competed as a member and captain of the club snowboarding team. Throughout my high school and college careers I worked part-time my family's farm, and spent a year working there full-time before starting graduate school. Outside of the lab, I enjoy rock climbing, snowboarding, cycling, and running.
