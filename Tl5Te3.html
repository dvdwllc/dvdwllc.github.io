Topological insulating state in Tl5Te3

Strong 3-dimensional topological insulators (3DTI's) are a newly discovered class of materials whose surfaces are exceptionally conductive and host unique electron transport properties which were once thought only to be accessible under large magnetic fields. Theorists have predicted a wide variety of potential applications for 3DTI's, such as quantum computation, but experimental work in this field is still in its infancy. In this publication we report the discovery of a new 3DTI, thallium telluride, and explore its topological surface states through computation and experiment. We further show that these surface states likely coexist with superconductivity at low temperatures, making this thallium telluride a good candidate material in which to study the complex interactions between topological surface states and superconductivity.
